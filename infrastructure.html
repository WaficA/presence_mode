Sovereign Infrastructure

Own the stack.
Don't rent it.

Self-hosted compute, network, and storage — designed, built, and operated on hardware owned outright. No hyperscaler, no vendor lock-in, no third party between an organisation and its own systems.

What this is.

A complete infrastructure stack that an organisation owns rather than leases: virtualization, segmented networking, and distributed storage, built from the hardware up. It is the same foundation Fenrir runs on — generalised for operators, enterprises, and regulated environments that cannot put their systems on someone else's cloud.

Air-gapped-capable by design: it can run fully disconnected from the public internet, for environments where that is a requirement rather than a preference.

Built to a standard, not a slogan.

We hold a hard line between what is built and what is designed — and we tell you which is which. Our bar for "done" is cold-reboot verification: a service is finished only when the machine can be powered off cold, powered back on, and recover on its own with nothing started by hand.

Proven in the hardest version of the problem.

The demanding case isn't a managed cloud with a one-command installer — it's standing up a full stack by hand, air-gapped, with no internet and no lifecycle automation doing the work for you: your own offline package repositories, databases, services, and network fabric, brought up from bare metal. That is the version that forces real understanding of every layer, and it is work we have done and validated in real air-gapped environments.

A published reference blueprint of this work is available on request.

Who it is for.

Telecom operators, enterprises, government and regulated bodies, and any organisation that needs its infrastructure to be owned, auditable, and under its own control — not sitting on a hyperscaler with a compellable copy of the data and a bill that scales against every user. If sovereignty over your own systems is a requirement, this is the foundation for it.
